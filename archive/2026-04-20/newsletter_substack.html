Will Anderson Jr. Becomes Highest-Paid Non-QB in History

Will Anderson Jr. just signed a 3-year, $150 million extension with the Texans. $50 million per year. For a defensive end. Who was drafted three years ago.

https://twitter.com/AdamSchefter/status/2046158432987654321

Let that breathe. $50 million per year. That's more than some starting quarterbacks make.

https://twitter.com/StatMuse/status/2046159887456123456

Anderson has 30 sacks in three seasons. That's $5 million per career sack at this point. The Texans drafted CJ Stroud at #2 and Anderson at #3 in 2023. Now they have a $55 million quarterback on a rookie deal and a $50 million pass rusher. That's $105 million between two players who haven't finished their fourth seasons.

https://twitter.com/HaterReport/status/2046161234567890123

Houston front office explaining their salary cap strategy:
Anderson becomes the highest-paid non-quarterback in NFL history, surpassing Micah Parsons' $47 million AAV. The edge rusher market now mirrors the quarterback market. When you're paying someone $50 million per year, they get a no-trade clause and the right to choose where they play. The Texans are betting their championship window on having an elite quarterback-pass rusher combo locked up through 2029.

AJ Brown Is 'Likely' Becoming a Patriot

Adam Schefter doesn't use the word "likely" lightly. AJ Brown is heading to New England.

https://twitter.com/AdamSchefter/status/2046174326194356373

https://twitter.com/AdamSchefter/status/2046174911408881688

The Eagles are finally ready to move on after all the sideline drama and contract disputes from last season. The Patriots desperately need a WR1 and have cap space for a salary dump trade post-June 1. Patriots fans who have been watching DeVante Parker and JuJu Smith-Schuster as their WR1 options:
The timing is everything. June 1 designations allow the Eagles to spread Brown's cap hit over multiple years, making the trade financially viable for Philadelphia. Brown gets to be the clear WR1 on a team that needs him, the Eagles get cap relief and draft capital, everyone wins except for the drama Twitter accounts who lose content.
