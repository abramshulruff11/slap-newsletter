Wemby Wins DPOY at 21, Drops 35 in Playoff Debut

Victor Wembanyama just became the youngest Defensive Player of the Year winner in NBA history. He's 21 years old.
@ShamsCharania
San Antonio Spurs' Victor Wembanyama has won the 2025-26 NBA Defensive Player of the Year award. He is the youngest player in league history to win the award.
View tweet
The previous record holder was Dwight Howard at 23. Wemby beat it by two full years. He's not just good — he's historically unprecedented. And just to make sure everyone understood what they were dealing with, he dropped 35 points in his playoff debut. That's a Spurs franchise record for a debut playoff game. Tim Duncan never did that. David Robinson never did that. The Big Fundamental himself never put up 35 in his first playoff appearance.
21 points in the first half alone — the most points scored in a debut playoff half since the play-by-play era began. The Spurs are back in the playoffs after the Duncan/Ginobili/Parker era ended. Their new franchise cornerstone just did something even the greatest Spur never accomplished. DPOY at 21. Franchise playoff scoring record in his debut. San Antonio found their next generational talent. The league is in trouble.

Brandon Aubrey Goes From Spring Football to NFL History
