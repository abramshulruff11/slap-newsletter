Wemby's Face-First Fall Throws Spurs Season Into Chaos

Victor Wembanyama fell face-first to the court Monday night and everything changed. The 7-foot-4 superstar — fresh off becoming the youngest and first unanimous Defensive Player of the Year winner — spun around Jrue Holiday in the paint, got fouled, and couldn't brace his fall. His jaw hit the hardwood with 8:57 left in the second quarter. Wemby stayed down for 30 seconds. Sat up for a minute talking to teammates. Then jogged to the locker room and didn't return.

Fernando Mendoza Won't Attend Draft to Care for Mom

Fernando Mendoza is staying home for the NFL Draft. The likely #1 pick is skipping the green room to be with his mother, Elsa, who has battled multiple sclerosis for over 15 years. He wants to make the memory with everyone who poured into his football journey — mentors, coaches, family, friends. Still expected to go first overall to the Raiders on Thursday. Just from his living room instead of Broadway.
