Diego Pavia Goes Undrafted Then Gets Ravens Minicamp Invite

Diego Pavia — Heisman Trophy finalist, SEC Offensive Player of the Year, guy who led Vanderbilt to their first 10-win season in program history — went completely undrafted yesterday.

This is the first Heisman finalist to go undrafted since Jordan Lynch from Northern Illinois in 2014. Lynch never made an NFL roster. The NFL's reasoning: 5'9 7/8" at the combine, arm strength questions, and a polarizing personality that scared front offices. About that personality. Pavia posted "F all the voters" after losing the Heisman to Fernando Mendoza. He sued the NCAA for eligibility and won. He trespassed on rival New Mexico's practice field and urinated on it. His brothers repeatedly drew law enforcement attention at Vanderbilt games.
But here's what the NFL is potentially missing: 3,539 passing yards, 29 TDs, 70.6% completion percentage. Plus 862 rushing yards and 10 rushing TDs. He took Vanderbilt from 2 wins to 10 wins in one season. Beat Tennessee at Neyland for the first time since 1982. Teams looking at Pavia's resume vs his height measurement:

Rams Draft Stafford's Replacement While He's Still the Reigning MVP

The Rams took Alabama quarterback Ty Simpson with the 13th overall pick. Matthew Stafford is 38 years old, coming off an MVP season, and still has two years left on his contract. Sean McVay admitted he had a conversation with Stafford about the pick but "wouldn't divulge what was said." That's not awkward at all.

Nigeria's Uar Bernard Gets Drafted Having Never Played Football

The Eagles used a 7th round pick on Uar Bernard from Nigeria. Bernard has never played organized football. Meanwhile, SEC Offensive Player of the Year Diego Pavia went completely undrafted.

Mike Tomlin: Recent Playoff Failures Drove My Decision to Step Away

Mike Tomlin made a rare admission about his departure from Pittsburgh. He stepped away "in part, because of the recent lack of playoff success" and believed the veterans were "worthy of the excitement and the optimism associated with new leadership." Translation: I wasn't getting it done, and these players deserved better.
You don't see coaches directly cite their own failures as the reason for leaving very often. Most give you the standard "pursue other opportunities" line. Tomlin just said the quiet part out loud.
