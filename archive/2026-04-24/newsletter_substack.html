Fernando Mendoza: From Cal Benchwarmer to #1 Overall Pick in 12 Months

The Raiders just drafted Fernando Mendoza first overall. One year ago, he was riding the bench at Cal.
@StatMuse
Fernando Mendoza: — Went Undefeated — Won National Championship — Won Heisman Trophy — Went 1st-Overall in NFL Draft One of four QBs to reach all of those accomplishments.
View tweet
The transfer portal success story to end all transfer portal success stories. Mendoza started 2025 as a backup quarterback at Cal, transferred to Indiana, won the Heisman, went undefeated, captured the national championship, and got drafted #1 overall. That's not a career arc — that's a fever dream. Only the fourth quarterback since 1967 to win the Heisman, national championship, AND go first overall. He joins Cam Newton, Joe Burrow, and Baker Mayfield in that exclusive club. But none of those guys made the jump from backup to #1 pick in one season.
@StatMuse
Raiders last three first-round picks: 2024 — Brock Bowers 2025 — Ashton Jeanty 2026 — Fernando Mendoza Offensive firepower.
View tweet
The Tom Brady connection is real. Mendoza had a FaceTime call with Brady before the draft, and Brady — now Raiders co-owner — promised to mentor whoever they selected. When Mendoza was six years old, he said he wanted to be "best friends" with Tom Brady. Dreams do come true. Mendoza didn't attend the draft in Pittsburgh. He stayed home in Miami with his family, watching from the couch like the rest of us. When his name got called, he gave his mother the longest hug in draft history.

"Hey Matt, you just won MVP, but we're using a first-round pick on your replacement. Thoughts?" Simpson started only 15 college games at Alabama. Most analysts projected him as a Day 2 pick.
