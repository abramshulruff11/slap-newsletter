Giants Trade Dexter Lawrence to Bengals for 10th Overall Pick in Draft Week Blockbuster

Draft week blockbuster: The Giants are trading three-time Pro Bowl defensive tackle Dexter Lawrence to the Bengals in a deal that will include the 10th overall pick going to New York, per ESPN sources.

The Giants now head into Thursday night with the 5th and 10th overall picks. pic.twitter.com/cV6Qobp6Aq

— Adam Schefter (@AdamSchefter) April 19, 2026
The Bengals just did something they haven't done since 1966. They traded a top-10 pick for a player.

A Bengals trade for Dexter Lawrence is unprecedented: This is the first time in the common draft era that dates back to 1966 in which the Bengals ever have traded a Top-10 pick for a player, per ESPN research. pic.twitter.com/RNsy7MbHqb

— Adam Schefter (@AdamSchefter) April 19, 2026
Let that sink in. The franchise that drafted Ki-Jana Carter over Warren Sapp, Akili Smith over Edgerrin James, and John Ross over Alvin Kamara just decided the 10th pick in a weak draft class is worth less than a defensive tackle with 0.5 sacks last season.
The Giants are sitting pretty with picks 5 and 10 in Thursday's draft. John Harbaugh gets to remake this defense with two lottery tickets in the first round. New York's NFL teams now control 25% of the top 16 picks — Giants at 5 and 10, Jets at 2 and 16. Meanwhile, the Bengals are going full desperation mode. They missed the playoffs three straight years and decided the solution was to trade their future for a guy who just had his worst season since his rookie year.

Absent a trade back info the first round, this will mark just the second time in franchise history the Bengals will not make a first round pick.

The only other instance was 1989.

— Field Yates (@FieldYates) April 19, 2026
1989. The year the Berlin Wall fell and the Bengals last operated without a first-round pick. They're betting Lawrence can single-handedly fix a defense that added Jonathan Allen, Boye Mafe, and Bryan Cook this offseason and still gave up 28 points per game.

John Harbaugh after stealing half the Ravens roster and then sending Dexter Lawrence to their division pic.twitter.com/B8QrsMtkzM

The financial gymnastics are wild. The Giants save $13.04 million in cap space but eat $13.92 million in dead money. The Bengals were 31st in cap space before this trade and just committed $42 million over two years to a player who went from 12 sacks in 2024 to half a sack in 2025.

This is either brilliant or the kind of desperation trade that gets front offices fired. Thursday night will tell us if Harbaugh just played chess while the Bengals played checkers.

Will Anderson Jr. Becomes Highest-Paid Non-QB Ever with $150M Extension

Will Anderson Jr. just broke the bank. Three years, $150 million with $134 million guaranteed. That's $50 million per year — QB money for a pass rusher. He joins nine quarterbacks in the $50-55 million range and stands alone as the only non-QB at that level. Anderson finished second in DPOY voting with 12 sacks and 20 tackles for loss. The Texans now have to pay their quarterback at least $50 million per year or admit their pass rusher is worth more than their franchise player. That C.J. Stroud extension conversation should be interesting.
