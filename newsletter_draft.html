The federal government just stepped into college athletics with both feet. President Trump signed a 74-page executive order Friday that would limit college athletes to five years of eligibility and one undergraduate transfer.

Cooper Flagg Becomes First Teenager to Score 50 in NBA Game

Cooper Flagg dropped 51 points last night and became the first teenager in NBA history to score 50. At 19 years, 103 days old, he broke Brandon Jennings' previous record by more than a year.

Cooper Flagg dropped 51 Points and became the first teenager in HISTORY to drop 50 in the NBA

• Flagg (19-103): 51
• Jennings (20-052): 55
• LeBron (20-080): 56
• Booker (20-145): 70
• Wemby (20-314): 50 pic.twitter.com/93h3pBQhWj

— Hater Report (@HaterReport) April 4, 2026
The historic part: this puts Flagg in Hall of Fame company. Only eight rookies have ever scored 50 — Kareem, Rick Barry, Elgin Baylor, Wilt, Elvin Hayes, Allen Iverson, Earl Monroe, and now Flagg. That's literally a Hall of Fame list plus Cooper Flagg. The less historic part: it happened in a blowout loss. The Mavericks are on their 14th straight home loss. Flagg scored 24 points in the fourth quarter of garbage time while his team got demolished.
