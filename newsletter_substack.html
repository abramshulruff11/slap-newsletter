Trump Signs Executive Order to Overhaul College Sports

The federal government just stepped into college athletics with both feet. President Trump signed a 74-page executive order Friday that would limit college athletes to five years of eligibility and one undergraduate transfer.

https://twitter.com/AdamSchefter/status/2040179404966342921

This isn't just sports policy. This is using federal funding as a weapon. Schools that don't comply lose federal money. The same playbook Trump used on DEI policies and transgender athletics — follow the order or lose the cash.

https://twitter.com/DanWetzel/status/2040176463551324571

The timing is perfect political theater. Final Four Saturday. March Madness at its peak. Every college sports fan in America is watching basketball and here comes the federal government with new rules.
Nick Saban's influence is all over this thing. Trump held a White House roundtable with him in March, and suddenly we get federal intervention in college sports. The man retires from Alabama and immediately becomes a policy advisor.

Cooper Flagg Becomes First Teenager to Score 50 in NBA Game

Cooper Flagg dropped 51 points last night and became the first teenager in NBA history to score 50. At 19 years, 103 days old, he broke Brandon Jennings' previous record by more than a year.

https://twitter.com/HaterReport/status/2040282809537851886

The historic part: this puts Flagg in Hall of Fame company. Only eight rookies have ever scored 50 — Kareem, Rick Barry, Elgin Baylor, Wilt, Elvin Hayes, Allen Iverson, Earl Monroe, and now Flagg. That's literally a Hall of Fame list plus Cooper Flagg. The less historic part: it happened in a blowout loss. The Mavericks are on their 14th straight home loss. Flagg scored 24 points in the fourth quarter of garbage time while his team got demolished.

https://twitter.com/StatMuse/status/2040235792367333512

The Rookie of the Year race just got interesting. Kon Knueppel still leads with 36 twenty-point games, but Flagg's historic night shifts the narrative.

https://twitter.com/StatMuse/status/2040235890023260533

Jason Kidd and his assistant got ejected defending Flagg on a no-call. The crowd booed when he was taken out at 45 points. Even in a terrible loss, everyone wanted to see history.
