DOJ Declares War on NFL Sunday Ticket Prices

https://twitter.com/NFLMemes/status/2042275256774119719

The Department of Justice opened an investigation into the NFL over Sunday Ticket pricing. They're going after the league's $111 billion, 11-year media rights deal that forces fans to spend nearly $1,000 annually on cable and streaming subscriptions to watch games. The NFL has antitrust exemption for broadcast TV deals under the 1961 Sports Broadcasting Act. But that doesn't cover streaming. Sunday Ticket costs $240 per year on top of everything else you're already paying. Every NFL fan looking at their monthly streaming bills right now:
MEME: Math Lady | Top: "Netflix + Hulu + ESPN+ + Peacock + Paramount+ + Sunday Ticket" | Bottom: "= Why do I need a second mortgage to watch football"
The league says 87% of games are on broadcast TV and calls their distribution "most fan-friendly in sports." Meanwhile, Senator Mike Lee is writing strongly-worded letters about paywalls and the FCC Chair is raising concerns about accessibility.
