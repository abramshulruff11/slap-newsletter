Will Anderson Jr. Signs Historic $150M Deal, Highest-Paid Non-QB Ever

Will Anderson Jr. just became the first non-quarterback to crack $50 million per year.
@AdamSchefter
Source: Will Anderson is signing a three-year $150 million extension that makes him the highest paid non-quarterback in NFL history. The deal includes $134 million guaranteed.
View tweet
Read that again. A defensive end is making quarterback money.
@AdamSchefter
Will Anderson Jr. in three NFL seasons: 🏈Defensive Rookie of the Year 🏈2x Pro Bowler 🏈First-Team All-Pro 🏈30 sacks 🏈4 forced fumbles 🏈3 fumble recoveries 🏈64 QB hits And now: the NFL's first-ever $50M-per-year non-QB.
View tweet
For context, only nine quarterbacks in the entire NFL make $50 million or more per year. Anderson just joined that club as a pass rusher.
@AdamSchefter
Highest paid non-quarterbacks in the NFL: 🏈Will Anderson Jr.: $50M 🏈Micah Parsons: $46.5M 🏈Aidan Hutchinson: $45M 🏈Jaxon Smith-Njigba: $42.15M 🏈TJ Watt: $41M 🏈Ja'Marr Chase: $40.25M
View tweet
Anderson is making $8 million more per year than the next highest non-QB. That's not a raise — that's a different tax bracket. Here's what just happened: the Texans accidentally set the floor for C.J. Stroud's extension at $60 million per year. You cannot pay your pass rusher $50 million and then tell your franchise quarterback to take less. The math doesn't work. The optics are worse.
@FieldYates
The Texans made the 3rd pick in the 2022 and 2023 NFL drafts. 2022: CB Derek Stingley, Jr. who signed a $30M/year extension last year, largest ever for a DB 2023: DE Will Anderson, Jr. who just signed the largest non-QB contract ever at $50M/year
View tweet
The Texans are about to pay their 2022-2023 draft class $110 million per year combined. That's what happens when you nail back-to-back draft picks and they both turn into superstars by age 24.
But here's the thing — this deal makes perfect sense. Anderson has 30 sacks in three seasons, finished second in DPOY voting last year, and he's only 24. Elite pass rushers break games. They're worth quarterback money because they can neutralize quarterbacks. This contract is going to age beautifully. Anderson in his prime, locked up through 2029, with a no-trade clause. The Texans just secured their defensive centerpiece for the next six years. Stroud's agent just moved his asking price from $55 million to $65 million.

Warriors Dynasty Officially Over as Kerr Acknowledges 'Expiration Date'

The Warriors dynasty might have breathed its last breath Friday night.

Steve Kerr to Steph and Draymond at the end of the Suns game: "I don't know what's gonna happen next, but I love you guys to death."
View tweet
When the greatest coach-core combination in modern NBA history is saying goodbye on a random Friday in April, you know something seismic is shifting.

Steve Kerr acknowledged after Friday's elimination his time with the Warriors could be done. He will take some time before a decision with management. "These jobs all have an expiration date." ESPN story

"These jobs all have an expiration date." That's not coach speak. That's a man who feels his time ending. When reporters asked what he said to Curry and Draymond:
@HaterReport
Steve Kerr when asked about the emotional moment at the end of the game with Draymond Green and Steph Curry: "None of your business" (h/t @ohnohedidnt24)
View tweet
The smile that came with "None of your business" said everything. Those were private words between three men who built something special together and watched it end. Twelve years, six Finals, four championships, 604 wins. The numbers tell one story. The embrace tells another.
This isn't just about missing the playoffs for the fourth time in seven years. This is about Curry at 36, Draymond at 35, and a coach who's watched his core get old in real time. The brutal math: this core won 73 games in 2016 and went 37-45 this season.
Kerr's decision comes down to this: coach a rebuilding Warriors team that might not contend again, or walk away at the peak of his legacy. He's 61. He's won everything. He doesn't need to suffer through a three-year roster teardown. If he leaves, the Splash Brothers era officially ends. Either way, the dynasty is over. The only question is whether they admit it.
