Michael Malone Leaves NBA Championship for $50 Million College Experiment

@JonRothstein
Michael Malone has officially signed a six-year contract worth more than $50 million dollars plus incentives to be the next head coach at North Carolina, per release.
View tweet
UNC just paid $8.3 million per year for an NBA champion coach. That makes Michael Malone the second-highest paid public school coach in America, behind only Bill Self at Kansas. The Nuggets fired Malone last spring with less than a week left in the regular season. He won them their first NBA championship in 2023 and was their all-time winningest coach with 510 regular season wins. Then Denver decided they needed a different voice.

"I have a chance to be a part of something special. ... This was the only college job I'd ever consider." Michael Malone on his decision to become the head coach of @UNC_Basketball.

His daughter Bridget is a sophomore volleyball player for the Tar Heels. This wasn't just about money — it was about family and timing. UNC also committed $6.75 million annually in revenue sharing to men's basketball. They're going full pro model after bringing in Bill Belichick. Two championship coaches from professional sports, both getting paid like professional coaches. Malone hasn't coached college basketball since 2001. But he has 44 postseason wins — 20 more than any other coach in Denver history. Now he gets to see if NBA championship pedigree translates to Chapel Hill.
