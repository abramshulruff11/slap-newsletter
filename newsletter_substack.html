Fernando Mendoza Goes From Cal Backup to #1 Overall Pick in One Transfer

Fernando Mendoza was the #134 quarterback recruit in Florida. Sat on Cal's bench. Transferred to Indiana where his brother already played. Now he's the consensus #1 overall pick in the 2026 NFL Draft.
@AdamSchefter

The statistical dominance is absurd. 70.8% completion rate with a 9.5-yard average depth of target — he wasn't padding stats with screens. 3,535 yards and 41 touchdowns in 2025. 58.7% of his dropbacks were successful plays, which ranks in the 99th percentile.
This is the Joe Burrow story all over again. Both were unknowns heading into their final college season. Both won the Heisman and national championship with the highest passer rating in FBS. Both operated like elite point guards, distributing all over the field before picking spots to hit daggers. The only difference? Burrow transferred from Ohio State to LSU. Mendoza transferred from Cal to Indiana and led the Hoosiers to their first-ever national championship. Raiders fans realizing they're about to draft a Cal backup:
Mendoza won't even attend his own draft. He's staying home in Miami, which would make him the first #1 overall pick to skip the ceremony since Trevor Lawrence in 2021. The timeline is genuinely insane: - 2022: #134 QB recruit, barely recruited - 2023: Cal backup, 1,708 yards as starter - 2024: 3,004 yards at Cal, decent but unspectacular - 2025: Transfers to Indiana, wins Heisman and national title - 2026: #1 overall pick The QBASE analytical model actually grades him lower than Trevor Lawrence and Caleb Williams because of the "one-year wonder" penalty. NFL teams don't care. He's the only quarterback with a first-round grade in this entire class.
